#+title:      Gemma 4 E4B chosen over Qwen 3.5 for local conversational voice
#+date:       [2026-04-18 Sat 13:05]
#+filetags:   :decision:voice:openwebui:model:
#+identifier: 20260418T130540

For Open WebUI Call-mode voice chat (local-only, Norwegian/English), picked `unsloth/gemma-4-E4B-it-GGUF:Q4_K_M` (~5 GB) over Qwen 3.5 4B.

**Why:**
1. Norwegian support. Gemma 4 pre-trained on 140+ languages with 35+ out-of-the-box. Qwen 3.x multilingual strength is CJK + English; Scandinavian is weaker.
2. Arena AI chat-preference in the 4B class favours Gemma 4 over Qwen 3.5. Qwen wins raw MMLU/HumanEval; Gemma wins "does it feel good to talk to" — which is what voice UX needs.
3. Native audio encoder in E4B (ASR + multi-turn conversation tuning). llama.cpp doesn't yet pipe audio directly in (OWUI uses Whisper → text → LLM), but it's a future path.
4. Fits alongside Whisper (1.5 GB) + Kokoro (0.3 GB) on the same GPU with headroom, unlike the 27–30B models that caused CUDA OOM.

**llama-swap entry:**
```yaml
gemma4-voice:
  cmd: llama-server --port ${PORT} -hf unsloth/gemma-4-E4B-it-GGUF:Q4_K_M -c 8192 --jinja
  ttl: 120
  aliases:
    - "gemma4:e4b"
    - "voice"
```

**How to apply:** Pick Gemma 4 E4B as the default when a) voice UX is primary, b) Norwegian/European languages matter, c) GPU must be shared with STT/TTS. Pick Qwen 3.5 small if pure English + tool-calling reliability matters more — Qwen is more consistent at structured function calls.
